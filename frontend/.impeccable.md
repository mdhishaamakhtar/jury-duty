## Design Context

### Users
Both researchers/academics and product teams self-hosting annotation workflows. Used in a focused, analytical context ‚Äî someone reviewing content items one at a time, making deliberate labeling decisions. Task-focused, not exploratory. Users are technically literate. The interface should get out of the way.

### Brand Personality
Thoughtful, editorial, deliberate. Feels like a well-designed research instrument ‚Äî a journal submission system or an archival database. Not playful or flashy. The serif wordmark reinforces a scholarly, trustworthy tone.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode on warm off-white (`#fafaf9`). Rose/red is the single accent color, used sparingly for primary actions and key dividers. Warm stone neutrals everywhere else. Typography pairs Source Serif 4 (brand/wordmark) with IBM Plex Sans (UI). The feel is refined and focused ‚Äî purposeful typography and whitespace carry the design.

**Anti-references**: No dashboard-style card grids with shadows everywhere. No dark mode with glowing accents. No bright multi-color palettes. No busyness competing with the content being labeled.

### Design Principles
1. **Typography carries the weight** ‚Äî the wordmark in Source Serif 4 is the brand; let type do the visual work rather than decoration
2. **Rose accent is precious** ‚Äî `#e11d48` appears only on primary actions, key dividers, and focused states; never decoratively
3. **Warm neutrals, not cold grays** ‚Äî all text, borders, and surfaces use stone tones; never reach for plain gray
4. **Space signals importance** ‚Äî generous whitespace above section headers; tight grouping within
5. **Content-first on mobile** ‚Äî labeling cards at full width; no chrome competing with what's being labeled
